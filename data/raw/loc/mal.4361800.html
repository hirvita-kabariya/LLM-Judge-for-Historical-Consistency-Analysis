The State of West Virginia, Supreme Court of Appeals Wheeling, March 18, 1865 [*?*] [*H*] To the President & Atty Genl of the U.S. Learning that E. M. Norton Marshal of the District Court of the United States for the District of West Va. has - resigned, or contemplates resigning the office of Marshal - it affords me pleasure to recommend to the favorable consideration of - your Excellencies - as Mr Nortons successor Samuel McCullough Esqr. of Wheeling - Mr. McCullough is thoroughly loyal - has served over three years in the U. S. Army with honor to himself, evidenced and acknowledged by promotions - has been reared in the Clerk's office of his County and is familiar with business - is a good businessman, and will fill the office I am sure, to the satisfaction of all. I take the liberty of troubling you with this letter, because of information [*43618*] [*[ac9555]*] that there are, or will be applicants for the position, who, in this latitude, are known as Copper heads - or Conservative. - Constitutional men - We in this region want & need only thoroughly loyal sources in official positions. And in our US. Courts we cant afford any farther dilution of the true elements - or what should be a power in the land for good may prove only a dead weight or even an engine of Evil - I trust you may be pleased to appoint Mr. McCullough to the position of Mr. Nortons successor as Marshal. - Yours truly E. B. Hall Atty. Genl. of W. Va [*43618 B/4 43612*] Transcribed and reviewed by contributors participating in the By The People project at crowd.loc.gov.
